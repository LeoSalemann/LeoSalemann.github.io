Inches or MiliMeters?

I started by charging off in inches, mostly based of the bed sizes of the epilog lasers. The thought occurs my careful notch work from Assingment 3 (Airfoil Lamp) was in millimeters. Also I'm having scaling problems when I go Adobe Illustrator into Rhino anyway. Grashopper Airfoil generator wants millimeters, Dremel 3D wants milimeters, Adobe Illstrato can be converted. Take a deep breath, go back to Illustrator, re-do my dimentions in mm.

Configure Rhnio for millimeters. Redo the Illustrator -> Rhino workflow. Measure wing in Rhino; compare to AI.

Back to Illustrator, scale by 723/2053.59 = 0.352, re-export to SVG, re-import to Rhino and ...

721 mm. Not bad at all. Time to save, commit, push, then re-do the airfoil and spar generation.

Re-trace the fuselage in Rhino, using Control Point Curves as Usual.

Pull up the Dr. Paterson's airfoil generator from Assignment 3 , line up the airfoil from the Tomahawk plans and roll my own. Start with the 129mm chord length measured from the plans, and set the thicknness to 20/129 = 15.5% of chord. I can actually get pretty close.

That said, I'm gonna go a little bigger, so I have more room for ailerons and hopefully fowler flaps.

Bake the airfoil, move it over by the traced fuselage, line up for horizontal stabilizer. Here's my next "cheat." To avoid having to cut v-notches to prevent the elevator and rudder from interfereing, I'm going to just extend the chord so the elevator portion simply overhangs the vertical tail. If I set my rudder the right way, no interferance (I hope.)

On to the wing spars, regenerating for milimeters. Use the spar generator from Assingment 3 , but first think about cardboard thickness. In assingment 3, all the cardboard was the us postal stuff. This time, the airofils will still be the USPS thin cardboard, while the spars will be science fair trifold or somtehing similar. So, the slots cut into the spar will be thin, to accmodate the airofils. I can use the slot thicknesses from Assingment 3. The slots cut into the airofils will be thick, to accmodate the thick spar. I might be able to dig up the thickness from one of my early guage studies, but I can worry about that later. Important thing is I can use the Assingment 3 spar generator with its existing slot dimensions

Tunrs out the Spar generator actually operates in inches, but it kinda doesn't matter. I can prettymuch "eyeball it" to get the spart lengh to agree with the wing span on the original plan.

Similarly, I can compare it to the airfoil I just made to make sure the spar is narrow enough.

I gonna probalby hafta do some prelminary laser cuts pretty soon. But first imma thinken the spar, then just be more careful where I place along the airfoil profile.

Bake, and move on to the elevator spar. This one looks small enough that I can probalby use USPS cardboard, too.

Use an even number of airfoils, not odd. I had a plan to cut some lightening holes in the fuselage, and sort of "carve out" airfoil shapes to match the wing and horizontal stablizer. Forget that. Use an even number of airofils, then shape the fuselage with an "airfoil-shaped dent" with notches for the spars to slide in. Notches should only be on the fuselage side, so the wing can slide left and right as necessary for centering.
